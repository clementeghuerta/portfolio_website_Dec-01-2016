Beer Shop App
I developed the design for the “Beer Shop” App with the thought of having a great user interface that involved a fluid animation path from one point to another. My thought process behind this application was to be in someone’s position who would like to order beer for delivery. I wrote down and planned all of it before starting the design.
  1. My process for this design:
  2. Step 1:

    Step 1: Write down what I wanted from the app.

  3. Step 2:

    Write each option for each page down in different sections, which then formed each individual page.

  4. Step 3:

    Sketch the user interface with the options I had written down and then finalizing each sketch.

  5. Step 4:

    Design with Adobe Illustrator and choosing the best colors, which showed the same branding across as well as a appealing design for beer.

  6. Step 5:

    Prototype with inVision.

  7. Step 6:

    Re-adjust the design.

  8. Step 7:

    Finalize all of the pages.

Infographics

Summary of Series:

I began to create a series of Apple infographics, which showed how to company grew from the 1980’s to the present day in history and the reasons why.I titled this piece, " Infographic: Evolution of Logo + Computer + Net Income + Moore’s Law "

There is a relation I have made with the branding of the company, by which I mean the logo and computer stylizations as well as the how that has been influenced by the speed of computers–or in other words– the speed and growth of technology all influencing the net income of the company and their great user interfaces.



  1. My process for this design:
  2. concept and ideas:

    In this section of the process I conceptualized what I thought others like myself would like to learn about Apple. I had always wondered why their net worth is so high, so I thought to seek out why.

  3. Research:

    Research is alway a part of my design process and over the years I have gotten somewhat good at doing it. For this specific project, I mainly did stock related research and Apple tech specs reasearch also.

  4. Concept and layout of design:

    At this stage of the process, I sketched a couple of illustrations in thumnails then at a larger scale with side notes of what I thought needed more information.

  5. Final touches on Rough Draft
  6. Input from Others:

    I find myself, at times, a bit stuck on how to proceed with perfecting and symplifying my ideas to show information as clear as possible. For this project, I asked an art director to look at it and give me pointers.

  7. Final Clean-up:

    As for many designers, the last step in any design is to perfect everything, by which I mean make sure you are following the grid and branding across your design.

About

Hello, I am a designer based in the Silicon Valey.

Looking back at what made me want to become a designer, I remember that when I was a teenager I started to fall in love with art and many mediums within it. Illustration exponentially took over the time in which I created other art and did anything else for that matter. After high school, I attended Academy of Art University to concentrate in 2D and 3D illustration. I enjoyed it very much, for it was an extremely fun time as I learned many valuable skills such as how to storyboard an animation, and I even directed a group of students for a short story animation.

I believe that because I grew up with other great inspirations like Apple and later Facebook, this led me to follow my passion in graphic and web design.

And as I read more and more on web design and the many great job opportunities available such as User Interface designer, Visual Designer, and Product Designer, I have made it a goal to focus my skills in user interface design, responsive web design, and product design. Currently, I am a Senior at California State University East Bay as a graphic designer.

I have worked as a front-end developer, vector re-touch artist and illustrator. I believe that my strongest strength is planning out my designs, having great visual, written, and verbal communication skills, and working well within a team as well as with minimal to no direction.
